## 3. Training Schedule & Framework

### Weekly Volume Target

**Mallorca camp week (April 9–12):** ~16–20 hours expected — treat as peak load week, full recovery after

### Normal Weekly Schedule

| Day | Primary Session | Duration | Notes |
|-----|-----------------|----------|-------|
| Sunday | Long ride (outdoor preferred) | 3–4 h | Z2 endurance, keep HR < 82% LTHR (< ~136 bpm) |
| Monday | Rest or active recovery | — | Walk, light movement only |
| Tuesday | Structured ride (ERG) | 1–2 h | Xert/Auuki session — threshold or VO₂ work |
| Wednesday | Endurance ride or split | 1–1.5 h | Z2 — no grey zone. Can split AM/PM if scheduling requires |
| Thursday | Structured ride (ERG) | 1–2 h | Sweetspot or threshold intervals |
| Friday | Rest or easy spin | 30–60 min | Full rest preferred before weekend |
| Saturday | Long ride (outdoor) | 2–3.5 h | Z2 endurance or terrain-paced |

> **Grey zone discipline:** If riding easy, ride easy (< 165W / HR < ~140 bpm). If it's a hard day, commit. Avoid the 170–195W "felt-good" drift that accumulates fatigue without adaptation stimulus.

### Session Details

| Session Type | Target Power | Duration | Purpose |
|--------------|--------------|----------|---------|
| VO₂Max | 233–264W (106–120% FTP) | 3–8 min efforts | Raise ceiling, improve aerobic power |
| Endurance | 123–165W (Z2) | 2–4 h | Aerobic base, fat oxidation, alpine durability |
| Sweetspot | 185–213W (84–97% FTP) | 20–40 min efforts | Efficient threshold development |
| Long Ride | 123–165W (Z2, HR-guided) | 3–5 h | Endurance, prepare for passes/Mallorca |
| Recovery | < 121W (Z1) | 30–60 min | Active recovery only — resist the urge to push |

### Recovery Protocol

**Recovery Triggers (Auto-Deload):**
- HRV ↓ > 20% from 7d baseline (93.2) → reduce intensity, Z1–Z2 only
- RHR ↑ ≥ 5 bpm from baseline (49 bpm) → deload session or rest
- Feel ≥ 4 (Fatigued) → replace hard session with easy spin or rest
- Two or more triggers → complete rest day, no training

**Feel Scale:**
| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Excellent (fully recovered) |
| 2 | Good (normal fatigue) |
| 3 | Moderate (manageable tiredness) |
| 4 | Fatigued (deload trigger) |
| 5 | Exhausted (complete rest required) |

## 5. Nutrition / Fueling

### During-Ride Fueling

No fixed recipe — uses commercial products (bars, gels). Pragmatic approach is fine; consistency and quantity matter more than the source.

**Rough targets to aim for:**

| Workout Type | Duration | CHO Target | Practical Setup |
|--------------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Recovery / Z1–Z2 | < 1.5 h | 0–30 g/h | Water only or 1 gel if hungry |
| Endurance | 1.5–3 h | 60 g/h | 2 gels/h or 1 bar + 1 gel per hour |
| Long Endurance | 3–5 h | 80–90 g/h | Mix bars + gels + real food; Mallorca = pizza stops count |
| Threshold / SS | 1–2 h | 40–60 g/h | Gel before + 1 per 30–45 min of hard work |
| VO₂ / High Intensity | 1–1.5 h | 30–60 g/h | 1–2 gels, small sip bottle with carbs |

### Recovery Nutrition

**Protocol:** Eat proteins + carbs as soon as possible post-ride. This is already correct behavior.
**Rough targets:** 0.3–0.4 g/kg protein + 1–1.2 g/kg carbs within 30–60 min of session end.
At 81 kg: ~25–30 g protein + 80–100 g carbs post-ride.

### Hydration

**Current gap:** Under-hydrating — focus on drinking more.
**Target:** 500–750 ml/h during rides (more in heat/effort).
**Sodium:** ~500–800 mg/L; add electrolytes on rides > 90 min, especially in summer/alpine heat.
**Daily baseline:** 2.5–3 L total fluid intake including off-bike.

## 9. Coach Notes

- **Grey zone discipline is the #1 priority.** Mic acknowledges the tendency to drift into Z3 (167–198W). Easy days must stay below 165W / HR ~140 bpm. Consider printing this on the stem: "Is this Z2?"
- **Strength training goal is not yet established** — this is a high-value add for alpine performance (hip stability, leg resilience on long climbs). Start with 2×/week if 3× feels like too much, and anchor it to fixed days (e.g., Mon/Thu).
- **Hydration needs active attention** — set a water reminder on the Karoo 3 (every 15 min) and start each ride with 2 full bottles minimum.
- **FTP may be underestimated.** eFTP of 247W suggests the 220W figure may be conservative. A ramp test or 20-min test after the Mallorca recovery would be timely to recalibrate zones.
- **Weight management:** 6 kg to target is meaningful but not urgent. Do not combine a caloric deficit with high-load weeks. Focus on quality of food rather than restriction.
- **Optical HRM limitation:** At threshold and above, lag can be 5–15 bpm. For structured intervals, HR data should be treated as indicative, not prescriptive.
